Getting a professional property valuation used to mean scheduling an appraiser, waiting days for a slot, and paying upwards of 1,500 PLN — with no guarantee the methodology would be more rigorous than your own research. Estify AI replaces that process with a three-step flow: you describe the property, our AI analyses it against 76 data sources, and a certified appraiser verifies the result before it reaches your inbox the next business day.

## The Three-Step Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Fill In the Property Form (~15 minutes)">
    Everything starts with a structured online form at [estify.ai](https://estify.ai). The form takes roughly 15 minutes to complete and asks for approximately 30 data points about the property. You don't need to be a real estate expert — the questions are straightforward, and each field includes guidance notes.

    **What the form covers:**

    - **Location** — Full address, district, and neighbourhood
    - **Size and layout** — Total floor area (m²), number of rooms, number of bathrooms
    - **Building details** — Building type (block of flats, tenement, detached house, etc.), total number of floors, floor the unit is on, year of construction
    - **Condition and finish** — Overall condition (new / good / needs renovation), standard of interior finish, recent renovations
    - **Amenities** — Balcony, terrace, garden, basement storage, lift, air conditioning
    - **Orientation** — Primary window orientation (south-facing units command a premium in Polish market data)
    - **Parking** — Underground garage, surface parking space, or none
    - **Heating** — District heating, gas central, electric, or other
    - **Legal details** — Land and Mortgage Register number (Księga Wieczysta), if available

    <Tip>
      Having your Land and Mortgage Register number (Księga Wieczysta) ready before you start speeds up the legal verification section and improves the accuracy of the report's encumbrance check.
    </Tip>

    You can save your progress and return to the form at any time before submitting.
  </Step>

  <Step title="AI Processing and Big Data Analysis">
    Once you submit the form, Estify AI's machine learning engine takes over. The model was developed in collaboration with the **Cracow University of Economics** and is trained on millions of Polish property transactions, continuously updated as new market data becomes available.

    The engine cross-references your property's attributes against **76 distinct data sources** to build a complete valuation picture. This isn't a simple price-per-square-metre multiplication — the AI identifies statistically significant comparables, weights them by recency and proximity, adjusts for your property's specific characteristics, and models the result against current market conditions.

    Processing typically completes within a few hours of form submission. The output is a structured valuation dataset that feeds directly into your report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Appraiser Verification and Report Delivery">
    Before your report is sent, a **certified real estate appraiser** (rzeczoznawca majątkowy) reviews the AI's output. The appraiser checks the methodology, validates the comparable selection, reviews the legal register findings, and signs off on the final valuation figure.

    This human review step is what distinguishes Estify AI from automated estimate tools. Your report carries the professional endorsement of a licensed appraiser — making it suitable for use in mortgage applications, legal proceedings, and investment documentation.

    Once approved, the completed report is delivered to your email address **by the end of the next business day**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The next-business-day delivery window applies to forms submitted on standard working days. Forms submitted on Fridays after 17:00, on Saturdays, Sundays, or on Polish public holidays will be processed on the next working day, with delivery the following business day. You'll receive a confirmation email immediately after payment with your expected delivery date.
</Note>

## Data Sources: Where the Numbers Come From

The 76 sources Estify AI draws on span every dimension of property value. They fall into five broad categories:

### Market Transaction Data
Real estate transaction registers, notarial deed databases, and municipal price records provide the backbone of comparable sales. The AI works with both publicly disclosed and licensed private transaction datasets to ensure comparables are recent, local, and relevant to your property type.

### Legal and Register Data
The National Land and Mortgage Register (Elektroniczne Księgi Wieczyste), the Register of Prices and Values of Real Properties (RCiWN), and cadastral data from the Central Geodetic and Cartographic Documentation Centre (CODGiK) inform the legal verification sections of your report.

### Spatial Planning and Zoning Data
Local zoning plans (Miejscowy Plan Zagospodarowania Przestrzennego — MPZP), study documents, and development decisions from municipal offices establish what can be built nearby — a critical factor for long-term value forecasts and environmental risk assessments.

### Macroeconomic Indicators
Interest rate data from the National Bank of Poland (NBP), mortgage availability statistics, inflation indices, and household income data feed the price forecast models and investment return calculations.

### Infrastructure and Location Data
Public transport accessibility scores, proximity to schools and healthcare facilities, noise maps, flood risk zone data, and air quality indices round out the location risk and amenity analysis in your report.

## Accuracy: Within 2.3% of Market Value

Estify AI valuations achieve an average deviation of **2.3% from actual transaction prices** — validated against thousands of completed sales across Polish markets. This figure is independently benchmarked by the Cracow University of Economics research team and updated quarterly.

To put that in context: on a 600,000 PLN apartment, a 2.3% deviation means the valuation is accurate to within approximately 13,800 PLN in either direction. Traditional appraisals, which rely on a smaller set of manually selected comparables, frequently show larger deviations — without being any faster or more affordable.
