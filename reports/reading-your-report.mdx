Once you've placed your order, your Estify AI report does two things: it tells you what a property is worth, and it gives you the evidence to act on that knowledge. This guide walks you through how to read each section of your report and how to put it to practical use — whether you're buying, selling, investing, or advising a client.

## Report Delivery

Your report is delivered by email the next business day after your order is placed. The email will contain:

- A **PDF of your full report**, ready to save, print, or share
- A **summary of the key valuation figure** in the email body so you can see the headline result immediately
- For Pro customers: a **link to the decision-support tool**, accessible from any browser

If your report hasn't arrived by the end of the next business day, check your spam or junk folder first, then [contact support](#when-to-contact-support).

## Understanding the Valuation Figure

The valuation figure is the AI-calculated estimate of the property's current market value, accurate to within 2.3%. Here's how to read it:

- **Central estimate** — this is the primary number: what the AI determines the property is worth based on data from 76 integrated sources, including recent comparable transactions, local market trends, and property-specific attributes.
- **Confidence range** — shown as a band around the central estimate (e.g. 580,000–610,000 PLN). This reflects natural variation in market data. The tighter the range, the more comparable transaction data was available for your area.
- **Comparison to listing price** — where relevant, the report notes how the property's current asking price compares to the estimated market value, giving you an immediate read on whether it's priced fairly, above, or below market.

<Note>
The valuation reflects market value at the time the report is generated, based on the data you provided. It is not a legally binding appraisal document for mortgage or court purposes — for that use case, please consult a licensed appraiser directly.
</Note>

## Using Price Forecasts

### Basic Report

The Basic report includes a short-term price outlook for the local market. Use it to gauge the general direction of prices — rising, stable, or softening — and factor that into your timing decision.

### Pro Report: 20-Quarter Forecasts

The Pro report extends this into a full five-year, 20-quarter forecast with three macroeconomic scenarios:

- **Optimistic scenario** — assumes favourable conditions: economic growth, low unemployment, continued housing demand
- **Baseline scenario** — the most statistically likely outcome given current trends
- **Pessimistic scenario** — models a downturn, rising interest rates, or demand contraction

To read the forecast chart, look at the spread between the three lines. A narrow spread means the forecast is robust across scenarios; a wide spread signals higher uncertainty and warrants more caution. Pay particular attention to the **inflection points** — moments where the scenarios diverge — as these often correspond to forecasted macroeconomic events or seasonal market cycles.

## The Decision-Support Tool (Pro Only)

The decision-support tool is included with every Pro report and accessed via the link in your delivery email. It lets you input your own financial assumptions and model the outcome of different decisions.

You can use it to:

- **Set your target purchase or sale price** and see how it compares to the AI valuation
- **Model a financing scenario** — input your deposit, mortgage rate, and term to see projected monthly costs and equity growth
- **Simulate hold periods** — see projected portfolio value and cumulative returns after 1, 3, 5, or 10 years
- **Run a sensitivity check** — adjust rental income or sale price assumptions and immediately see the impact on ROI and ROE

The tool is designed to surface a clear recommendation based on your inputs and the report data. It won't make the decision for you, but it will give you a data-grounded framework for making it yourself.

## Using Your Report in Practice

### For Buyers

Your report gives you two things: **validation** and **leverage**.

- **Validating the asking price** — compare the listing price to the central valuation estimate. If the asking price is significantly above the AI estimate, you have evidence to push back.
- **Negotiation arguments** — use the market data summary and competition analysis to support a lower offer. Sellers respond better to data than to opinion. Citing appraiser-verified figures puts your offer on solid ground.
- **Timing your purchase** — use the price forecast to assess whether waiting could save money or whether prices are trending upward and acting quickly is advisable.

### For Sellers

Your report helps you **price accurately and defend your price**.

- **Setting a listing price** — anchor your asking price to the AI valuation rather than guessing. Overpriced listings sit on the market; underpriced ones leave money behind. The report gives you a defensible number.
- **Listing arguments** — share relevant excerpts from your report with prospective buyers to demonstrate the property's value. Investment indicators, location analysis, and comparable transaction data all support your asking price.

### For Investors

Focus on the **extended investment analysis** section (Pro):

- **ROI and ROE** — understand total and equity returns under your expected financing structure
- **Sensitivity analysis** — stress-test your assumptions. What happens to returns if rental prices drop 10%? What if the property takes three months to let?
- **Revenue and cost breakdown** — use the projected cash flows to build your own financial model or present to a co-investor or lender

### For Agents

The Pro report is a presentation-ready document you can share directly with clients:

- Use the **charts and visualisations** to walk a client through market positioning and pricing rationale
- Reference the **appraiser verification statement** to establish the report's credibility with buyers or sellers unfamiliar with Estify AI
- Use the **decision-support tool** in client meetings to model scenarios in real time and demonstrate advisory value

## Legal Section: Understanding the Księga Wieczysta Check

The legal verification section (extended in Pro) tells you about the property's formal legal status. Here's what the key findings mean:

- **Clean title** — no encumbrances, mortgages, or disputes are registered against the property. This is the expected state for a property ready to transact.
- **Mortgage registered** — the seller's bank has a charge on the property. This is normal if the seller has an outstanding loan, but it must be discharged before or at the point of sale.
- **Easements or rights of way** — third parties may have legal access rights over part of the property. Depending on the type and scope, this can affect use and value.
- **Ownership discrepancies** — the registered owner does not match the seller's representation. Always resolve this before proceeding.

<Warning>
The legal verification in your Estify AI report is an informational summary based on publicly available register data. It does not constitute legal advice. For complex legal situations — disputed ownership, unresolved encumbrances, inheritance cases — consult a qualified Polish property lawyer (*radca prawny* or *notariusz*) before transacting.
</Warning>

## Appraiser Verification

Every Estify AI report — Basic and Pro — carries a verification statement from a certified property appraiser (*rzeczoznawca majątkowy*). This means a licensed professional has reviewed the AI output and confirmed it is consistent with current market conditions and professional valuation standards.

<Note>
Appraiser verification adds independent credibility to the report. While the report is AI-generated and processed at scale, the appraiser's review ensures it meets professional standards and reflects real-world market realities that pure algorithmic models can miss.
</Note>
