Most issues with Estify AI can be resolved quickly without needing to contact support. This page walks you through the most common problems customers encounter — from a missing report email to an unexpected valuation figure — and shows you exactly what to do. If you work through the relevant section and still need help, our support team is always available.

## Report Not Received

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I haven't received my report yet">
    Start with these steps before contacting support:

    **1. Check your spam or junk folder.**
    Report emails are sent from an **@estify.ai** address. Some email providers — particularly corporate or university inboxes — may filter these into spam. Search your entire mailbox for "Estify" if you can't find the message in your main inbox.

    **2. Verify the email address you entered.**
    Log in to your Estify account at [estify.ai](https://estify.ai) and check the email address on your order. A single typo (e.g. `.con` instead of `.com`) will mean the report was sent to a non-existent address. If the address is wrong, contact us and we'll resend the report to the correct one.

    **3. Check the delivery timeline.**
    Reports are delivered by the end of the **next business day** — not the next calendar day. An order placed on Friday afternoon will be ready by the end of Monday. Orders placed on public holidays are processed on the following working day.

    **4. Still nothing after 2 business days?**
    If 2 full business days have passed and you've checked the above, email us at [hello@estify.ai](mailto:hello@estify.ai) with your order number and the property address. We'll investigate and resend your report promptly.

    <Note>
      If you created an Estify account during checkout, you can also download your report directly from the **My Orders** section — no need to wait for the email to reappear.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Payment Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My payment wasn't confirmed">
    If you completed checkout but didn't receive a payment confirmation email, don't panic — transactions occasionally take a few minutes to process.

    - **Check your bank account or card statement** to see whether the charge has gone through. If the amount has been debited, the confirmation email may simply be delayed; wait 10–15 minutes and check your spam folder.
    - **If the payment hasn't gone through**, return to [estify.ai](https://estify.ai) and retry the payment. Make sure your card details are entered correctly and that your bank hasn't blocked the transaction (some banks flag first-time online purchases).
    - If you were charged but never received an order confirmation after 30 minutes, contact us at [hello@estify.ai](mailto:hello@estify.ai) with your payment reference number so we can locate your order.

    <Note>
      You will not be charged twice for a failed transaction. If a payment attempt fails, the original authorisation is automatically released back to your account within 1–3 business days depending on your bank.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I'm having trouble with Tpay installments">
    Tpay installment payments involve a separate approval process managed by Tpay's lending partners.

    - If your installment application was **declined**, Tpay will notify you by email with a reason. Common causes include a credit check result or incomplete identity verification. You can retry with a different payment method (card, BLIK, or PayPal) to complete your Estify order immediately.
    - If the installment was **approved but your order hasn't been activated**, the confirmation may be processing. Wait up to 30 minutes, then check your Estify account. If the order still shows as unpaid, contact **Tpay support** first to confirm the transaction status, then forward the confirmation to us at [hello@estify.ai](mailto:hello@estify.ai).
    - For all questions about installment terms, repayment schedules, and early repayment, contact **Tpay directly** — Estify AI does not manage the installment agreement itself.

    <Tip>
      Save your Tpay order reference number when you complete the installment application. It makes it much easier to resolve any issues quickly.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My BLIK code expired or timed out">
    BLIK codes are valid for only **2 minutes** from generation. If the code expires before the transaction is confirmed, the payment will not go through.

    - Return to the checkout page and **generate a fresh BLIK code** in your banking app.
    - Enter the new code promptly — avoid switching apps or tabs before completing the payment.
    - If your bank app is slow to generate a code, make sure you have a stable internet connection and that your banking app is up to date.

    <Tip>
      Have your banking app open and ready before you reach the payment step to avoid the BLIK code expiring mid-checkout.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Form Submission Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The form won't submit">
    If clicking **Submit** doesn't do anything or shows an error, the most common cause is one or more required fields being incomplete or invalid.

    - Scroll back through the form — required fields that are empty or incorrectly filled are usually highlighted in red.
    - Make sure numeric fields (such as property area) contain only numbers without units (e.g. enter `65` not `65 m²`).
    - Check that you've selected a property type from the dropdown rather than typing it manually.
    - If the form still won't submit after addressing all highlighted fields, try refreshing the page and re-entering your details, or switch to a different browser. If the problem persists, contact us at [hello@estify.ai](mailto:hello@estify.ai) and describe the field or step where you're getting stuck.

    <Note>
      The form auto-saves your progress, so refreshing the page should not cause you to lose data you've already entered.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't find my Land Register (Księga Wieczysta) number">
    The Land Register number is **entirely optional** — you can submit the form and receive a full valuation without it. Leaving this field blank will not prevent your order from being processed.

    That said, providing the number improves the accuracy of the **legal verification** section of the Pro report, which checks for mortgages, encumbrances, ownership disputes, and easements. If you'd like to include it, here's where to find it:

    - On your **notarial deed** (akt notarialny) from when the property was purchased
    - In your **property documentation** from the developer or previous owner
    - Via the **Ministry of Justice's electronic Księga Wieczysta portal** at [ekw.ms.gov.pl](https://ekw.ms.gov.pl) — search by court and register number if you have a partial reference

    <Tip>
      The Księga Wieczysta number follows the format **XX/XXXXXXXX/X** (e.g. KR1P/00123456/7). If you have a number in a different format, double-check the source document.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The address autocomplete can't find my property">
    The address autocomplete draws from a national address registry, which is occasionally missing newer developments or recently renamed streets.

    - Try typing the **street name and number manually** without selecting from the autocomplete dropdown — the form accepts freetext addresses.
    - If the street name has changed recently, try the **old name** or an alternative spelling (e.g. with or without diacritical marks).
    - For properties on new housing estates, try entering the **post code** first and then the street name, which can help narrow the suggestions.
    - If the address genuinely can't be found, enter the nearest major street or landmark as a reference and add clarifying details in the property description field. You can also email us the correct address after submitting and we'll update the order.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Questions About Valuation Results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="My valuation seems lower or higher than I expected">
    It's natural to feel surprised if the Estify AI valuation differs from the price you had in mind. Here's why that can happen:

    - **Listing prices ≠ market value.** Properties are often listed at aspirational prices — especially in a cooling market. Estify AI is calibrated against **actual completed transactions**, not asking prices, so the valuation may be lower than what you see advertised nearby.
    - **Condition and features matter.** Two apartments with the same floor area in the same building can differ significantly in value based on fit-out condition, floor level, orientation, and extras like parking.
    - **Location micro-factors.** Our AI analyses noise levels, flood risk, proximity to schools and transport, and planned infrastructure — all of which affect value in ways that aren't always obvious from a street-level view.

    If you believe specific data in your report is factually incorrect (e.g. the wrong area or floor number was used), contact us at [hello@estify.ai](mailto:hello@estify.ai) and we'll review the inputs and, if necessary, rerun the valuation.

    <Note>
      A lower-than-expected valuation can actually work in your favour when negotiating a purchase price or challenging a bank's assessment.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I interpret the confidence interval in my report?">
    Your Pro report includes a **confidence interval** — a value range rather than a single number — to reflect the inherent uncertainty in any property valuation.

    - The **central estimate** is the AI's best single-point valuation based on all available data.
    - The **lower and upper bounds** represent the range within which the true market value is very likely to fall (typically at a 90% confidence level).
    - A **narrow range** (e.g. ±2–3%) means the AI found many comparable transactions and the property's characteristics are typical for the area — high confidence.
    - A **wider range** (e.g. ±8–10%) means fewer comparable sales were found, or the property has unusual features that make direct comparison harder.

    Use the central estimate for planning purposes and the range to understand how much room there is for negotiation or risk in an investment scenario.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My Estify valuation differs from a traditional appraisal I received">
    Small differences between an Estify AI valuation and a traditional appraisal are normal and don't mean either one is wrong.

    - **Data sources differ.** Estify AI uses real completed transaction data from 76 sources. Some traditional appraisers supplement transaction data with listing prices or use a narrower set of comparables.
    - **Timing matters.** If the two valuations were done weeks apart, market movements may account for the gap.
    - **Methodology varies.** Traditional appraisers may apply different weighting to property features or use income-based approaches for investment properties.

    If the difference is large (more than 10–15%), it's worth reviewing both reports side by side to identify the specific factors driving the gap. Our support team is happy to walk you through the Estify methodology if that helps.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
