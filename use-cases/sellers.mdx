Pricing your property correctly from the start is the single most important factor in a successful sale. List too high and buyers ignore you; list too low and you leave real money on the table. Estify AI gives you an independent, appraiser-verified valuation so you can set a price with confidence — and defend it when buyers push back.

## The Problem: Pricing Without Data Is a Gamble

Most sellers set their asking price based on what a neighbor sold for two years ago, what an agent suggests on a first visit, or what they feel the property is worth. All three approaches carry serious risk:

- **Underpricing** means leaving tens of thousands of złoty behind — money that should be in your pocket
- **Overpricing** means your listing stagnates, buyers lose interest, and you eventually accept less than you would have if priced correctly from day one
- **No evidence to back your price** means every buyer negotiation becomes a battle of opinions — and buyers show up with low offers knowing you have nothing concrete to counter with

The Polish real estate market is local, granular, and moves fast. You need data that reflects your specific property, your specific street, and today's market conditions — not a rough estimate.

## Your Step-by-Step Seller Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Order Your Report Before You List">
    Don't guess your asking price — find out what your property is actually worth before you publish your listing. Fill in the ~30-field online form with your property's details. The whole process takes about 15 minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Your Asking Price Based on the Valuation">
    When your report arrives the next business day, use the AI valuation as your pricing anchor. You can choose to list at, slightly above, or right at the valuation — but the key is you're working from certified data, not intuition.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Study the Competition Analysis">
    Review how comparable properties in your area are priced and how long they've been on the market. This tells you where your listing sits relative to the competition and helps you frame your price in the right context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prepare for Buyer Objections">
    Before the first viewing, read through the report's key sections so you're ready to address the questions buyers will ask: "Why is it priced this way?" "What do comparable properties sell for?" "What's the neighborhood trajectory?" You'll have answers backed by data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the Report in Negotiations">
    When a buyer makes a low offer, don't just counter — explain. Share the relevant sections of your Estify report. An appraiser-verified valuation carries far more weight than your agent saying "the property is worth more."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close at Fair Value">
    Sellers who enter negotiations with certified data consistently achieve prices closer to their asking price. You're not fighting from a position of uncertainty — you're defending a well-evidenced number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You're selling a 55 m² apartment in Kraków's Krowodrza district. You've had two viewings and both buyers have offered 30,000–40,000 PLN below your asking price of 680,000 PLN, claiming the market doesn't support your number.

You ordered an Estify Pro report before listing. Here's how you respond:

- The **Estify valuation is 675,000 PLN** — confirming your asking price is firmly grounded in reality
- The competition analysis shows no comparable property in the district has sold below 660,000 PLN in the last two quarters, and the most recent transaction was at 678,000 PLN
