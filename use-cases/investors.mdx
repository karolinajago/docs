## The Problem: Evaluating Investment Potential Without Complete Data

Most investors working in the Polish residential market are making decisions with incomplete information. Common gaps include:

- **No rigorous ROI/ROE calculation** that accounts for financing structure, costs, and realistic rental income
- **No sensitivity analysis** to test how returns hold up if interest rates rise, rents soften, or the sale takes longer than planned
- **No long-range price forecasting** grounded in macroeconomic scenarios rather than simple trend extrapolation
- **Blind spots on environmental and zoning risks** that could impair rental demand, resale value, or development potential
- **Legal uncertainty** around title, encumbrances, or planning restrictions that could derail an acquisition entirely

Each of these gaps is a risk. Estify addresses all of them in a single report.

## Understanding ROI and ROE in Your Estify Report

**ROI (Return on Investment)** in the Estify report measures total return relative to the full capital deployed — purchase price plus transaction costs plus capital expenditure. It gives you a clear picture of how efficiently the property generates returns on every złoty invested.

**ROE (Return on Equity)** measures return relative to only the equity portion of your investment, factoring in any mortgage financing. When leverage is involved, ROE diverges significantly from ROI — and that difference tells you whether the financing structure is working in your favor or eroding returns.

Both metrics are calculated using property-specific data and reasonable market assumptions, giving you a meaningful starting point for your own financial modeling.

## Sensitivity Analysis and Macroeconomic Scenarios

<Note>
  The sensitivity analysis in your Pro report tests how your investment returns respond to changes in key variables: rental income levels, vacancy rates, interest rate movements, and exit price assumptions. Rather than presenting a single "most likely" scenario, Estify shows you the range of outcomes — so you understand not just what the investment could return, but how resilient those returns are if conditions change. The 20-quarter forecast similarly presents baseline, optimistic, and conservative macroeconomic scenarios rather than a single projection line.
</Note>
