## Understanding ROI and ROE

**ROI (Return on Investment)** in real estate is typically expressed as gross rental yield:

```
ROI = (Annual Rental Income ÷ Purchase Price) × 100
```

For major Polish cities, a gross rental yield of **4–7%** is generally considered healthy. Warsaw's city centre tends to sit at the lower end (higher prices, strong demand), while cities like Łódź, Katowice, or Wrocław can push closer to 6–7% gross. Below 4% in a secondary location warrants careful scrutiny.

**ROE (Return on Equity)** tells a different story — it reflects how hard your own money is working:

```
ROE = (Annual Net Income ÷ Your Equity Invested) × 100
```

Because ROE only divides by your down payment rather than the full purchase price, mortgage financing can significantly boost this figure. A property with a 5% gross ROI purchased with a 20% down payment might yield a ROE of 10–14% depending on loan terms and net income after costs.

## Worked Example

Here's how the numbers play out for a typical investment scenario:

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Purchase price | 400,000 PLN |
| Down payment (20%) | 80,000 PLN |
| Mortgage amount | 320,000 PLN |
| Monthly rental income | 2,200 PLN |
| Annual rental income | 26,400 PLN |

**ROI (gross yield):**
```
26,400 ÷ 400,000 × 100 = 6.6%
```
This sits comfortably within the healthy range for Polish real estate.

**ROE (before debt service):**
```
26,400 ÷ 80,000 × 100 = 33%
```
This figure looks high because it ignores mortgage repayments. Once you subtract annual mortgage costs (estimated at approximately 18,000–20,000 PLN for a 25-year loan at current rates), your net annual return on equity falls to a more realistic **8–10%** — still strong compared to many alternative asset classes.

<Note>
  **Gross yield vs. net yield:** The ROI figure shown in your report is gross yield — it does not deduct taxes, maintenance, management fees, or vacancy periods. Net yield in Poland typically runs **1.5–2.5 percentage points lower** than gross yield once all costs are accounted for. The revenue/cost breakdown sub-section provides the net yield calculation.
</Note>

## Revenue and Cost Breakdown

The breakdown table in your report projects:

- **Rental income**: based on comparable rental listings in the immediate area, adjusted for property size and condition
- **Maintenance costs**: estimated at 0.5–1% of property value annually for well-maintained apartments
- **Property tax (podatek od nieruchomości)**: local authority rates applied to the property surface area
- **Management fees**: if applicable, typically 8–12% of monthly rental income for a property management agency
- **Vacancy allowance**: a conservative buffer, usually 5–8% of annual rental income, reflecting realistic occupancy rates

This produces a net cash flow figure you can compare directly against your mortgage payments.

## Sensitivity Analysis

The sensitivity analysis answers one critical question: *what happens if things don't go to plan?*

Estify models three price-shock scenarios:

| Scenario | Price Drop | Impact on ROE |
|---|---|---|
| Mild correction | −5% | Modest reduction in equity value; cash flow unaffected |
| Moderate correction | −10% | Equity cushion shrinks; LTV ratio rises |
| Severe correction | −20% | Tests whether equity remains positive; highlights refinancing risk |

Importantly, sensitivity analysis also models the effect on rental income if the local market softens, giving you a stress-tested cash flow picture — not just a capital value view.

<Tip>
  Use the sensitivity analysis to set your personal risk threshold before you make an offer. If a 10% price correction would push your equity close to zero or make your cash flow negative, you may want to negotiate harder on price, increase your down payment, or reconsider the location.
</Tip>

## Financial Leverage Analysis

Leverage is a double-edged tool. The financial leverage section of your report shows how different mortgage scenarios — varying your loan-to-value ratio from 60% to 90% — affect both your ROE and your monthly cash flow. Higher leverage boosts ROE in a rising market but accelerates losses in a falling one.

The report presents these scenarios in a side-by-side table so you can compare the risk-return profile at different financing levels before speaking to a bank or mortgage broker.

## How This Connects to the Price Forecast

The investment analysis does not exist in isolation. The financial model feeding your ROI and ROE calculations is calibrated using the same price trajectory data that drives the **price forecast section** of your report. If the base-case forecast projects 6% appreciation over the next five years, that uplift is factored into your total return calculation — not just your annual yield.

We recommend reading the investment analysis and price forecast sections together for a complete view of both income return and capital growth potential.
