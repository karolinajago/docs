Location is the single most durable driver of property value — it cannot be renovated, relocated, or upgraded after purchase. The location analysis section of your Estify report quantifies what "good location" actually means for a specific address, translating proximity to infrastructure and services into scored, comparable metrics.

## What Location Analysis Covers

The location section evaluates a property's surroundings across six infrastructure categories, measuring both walking and driving distances to the nearest facilities:

- **Education** — primary schools, secondary schools, and universities within a defined radius
- **Healthcare** — GP clinics, pharmacies, hospitals, and specialist medical facilities
- **Retail and services** — supermarkets, local shops, and service points
- **Green space** — parks, playgrounds, forests, and recreational areas
- **Public transport** — bus stops, tram stops, metro stations, and rail connections
- **Culture and lifestyle** — restaurants, cafés, cinemas, cultural venues, and fitness facilities

For each category, the report shows the distance to the nearest facility, the number of facilities within a 500 m and 1,000 m radius, and an accessibility score that combines both proximity and density.

## Why Location Matters for Valuation

Infrastructure quality has a direct, measurable effect on market value. Estify's valuation model incorporates location scoring as one of its core inputs — properties with stronger accessibility profiles command higher prices, and the model accounts for this systematically rather than leaving it to subjective judgment.

Some of the strongest documented effects in Polish urban markets include:

- Properties within **500 m of a metro station** in Warsaw, Kraków, or other cities with metro/rapid transit command a **5–8% premium** over otherwise comparable properties further away
- Proximity to **well-rated primary schools** in catchment areas can add **3–6%** to residential values
- Immediate adjacency to **major arterial roads** (noise, air quality) can reduce value by **2–5%**, which the environmental risk section of your report addresses separately

<Note>
  Location scoring feeds directly into Estify's valuation model. A property with an above-average accessibility profile will reflect that in the final estimated value — and the report shows you exactly which factors are contributing positively or negatively. This transparency lets you understand the "why" behind the valuation, not just the number.
</Note>

## How Scores Are Calculated

Each of the six infrastructure categories receives an individual accessibility score on a standardized scale. The scores weight both **distance** (shorter is better) and **density** (more facilities nearby is better), because a single bus stop 800 m away is a very different situation from five stops within 300 m.

The category scores are then combined into an overall **Location Accessibility Index** for the property. This index is presented alongside the property's position relative to other addresses in the same district, so you can see at a glance whether the location is above or below the neighborhood average.

## How Estify Sources Location Data

Location data in Estify reports is drawn from mapping databases, public infrastructure registries, and municipal facility records — all cross-referenced and updated regularly. Data sources include official Polish public records, transport operator schedules and stop databases, and commercial mapping infrastructure, aggregated from across Estify's network of **76 data sources**.

This means the distances and facility counts in your report reflect the real, current state of the area — not outdated or self-reported information.

## Practical Example

Two apartments of identical size and condition sit in the same Warsaw district, listed at similar prices. The Estify location analysis reveals a meaningful difference:

**Apartment A** is 350 m from a metro station, has four primary schools within 1 km, a large park within 200 m, and a supermarket on the ground floor of the building. Its Location Accessibility Index scores in the **top 15%** for the district.

**Apartment B** is 1.4 km from the nearest metro stop (bus-only), has one school within 1 km, and the nearest park is 900 m away. Its index scores in the **bottom 40%** for the district.

Despite similar asking prices, the location data gives you a clear, objective basis for evaluating whether the price premium on Apartment A is justified — and it almost always is.

## How to Use This Section

**For buyers:** Use the location scores to verify that the property matches your lifestyle needs and commute requirements before visiting. The section saves significant time by surfacing deals where asking prices don't reflect weak infrastructure — and confirming when a premium location genuinely earns its price.

**For sellers:** The location analysis highlights the positives you may take for granted as a current resident. Nearby metro access, a park, or a cluster of schools are genuine value drivers that justify your asking price — and this section gives you the data to back that up in conversations with prospective buyers.

<Tip>
  When comparing two properties at similar price points, run location analyses on both and compare their Location Accessibility Index scores side by side. A 5-point difference in the index often corresponds to a 3–7% difference in long-term value appreciation — making a seemingly identical choice much clearer.
</Tip>
