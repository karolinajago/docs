The competition analysis section shows you how a property stacks up against similar listings currently on the market. By benchmarking the subject property against real, active comparables in the same area, Estify gives you an objective picture of whether an asking price is realistic — or whether there's room to negotiate.

## What the Competition Analysis Shows

This section pulls together a curated set of comparable properties — referred to as *comparables* or *comps* — listed in the same neighborhood or district. For each set of comps, you'll see:

- **Number of similar active listings** in the area at the time of the report
- **Price per square meter (PLN/sqm)** for each comparable property
- **Median price/sqm** for the comparison group
- **Minimum and maximum price/sqm range** across comparables
- **Where the subject property sits** relative to that range — above, below, or at the median

This makes it immediately clear whether the property in question is priced in line with the market, commands a premium, or represents a potential bargain.

## How Estify Selects Comparable Properties

Not every nearby listing qualifies as a true comparable. Estify's selection algorithm filters properties across six key dimensions to ensure meaningful like-for-like comparisons:

| Criterion | What it controls for |
|---|---|
| **Proximity** | Distance from the subject property (street or sub-district level) |
| **Property type** | Apartment, house, studio — matched to the subject |
| **Size** | Floor area within a defined tolerance band around the subject's sqm |
| **Floor** | Ground floor, mid-floor, and top-floor properties are grouped separately |
| **Condition** | Developer standard, renovated, and unrenovated are not mixed |
| **Age** | Build decade — a 1970s block is not compared to a new-build |

By applying all six filters simultaneously, the comparables set reflects properties a real buyer would actually consider as alternatives to the subject property.

## Practical Example

Consider a 50 sqm apartment in Warsaw's Mokotów district. The competition analysis in its Estify report identifies **12 comparable listings** within the same sub-district, all within ±10 sqm of the subject property's size, on mid-level floors, in renovated condition, in buildings from the 1990s–2000s.

The results look like this:

- **Price/sqm range:** 12,000 – 14,500 PLN/sqm
- **Median price/sqm:** 13,200 PLN/sqm
- **Subject property asking price:** 14,100 PLN/sqm

At 14,100 PLN/sqm, the subject property sits in the **upper quartile** of the comparables range — above median, but not beyond the top of the market. The report flags this clearly, giving both buyers and sellers an immediate, data-backed reference point.

## How to Use This Section

**For buyers:** Compare the asking price directly against the median. If the property is priced significantly above the median without obvious justification — a better floor, a recent renovation, a premium view — that gap is a conversation starter. If it's priced at or below median, you have a data-backed reason to move quickly.

**For sellers:** Use the comparables set to sense-check your listing price before going to market. Pricing above the top of the range requires a clear differentiator; pricing near the median maximizes visibility and reduces time on market.

**For investors:** The active listing count matters as much as the price data. A high number of comparable listings signals supply pressure that may suppress price growth or require a price reduction to sell.

<Tip>
  Print or screenshot the competition analysis before entering a negotiation. Walking into a conversation with a data-backed median price — sourced from 12 live comparables in the same sub-district — is far more persuasive than referencing a general market estimate. Use the subject property's position in the range (upper quartile, below median, etc.) as your anchor point.
</Tip>
