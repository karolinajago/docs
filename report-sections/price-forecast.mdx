Knowing what a property is worth today is only part of the picture. Whether you're buying a home, selling an investment flat, or deciding whether to hold or exit a position, the direction of prices over the next few years matters enormously. The price forecast section of your Estify AI report models property value trajectories up to 20 quarters — five years — into the future, giving you a structured, evidence-based view of where prices in your specific location are likely heading.

## How Far Ahead Does Estify Forecast?

The forecast extends up to **20 quarters (5 years)** from the date of your report. This window is long enough to be meaningful for investment planning and mortgage term considerations, while remaining within the range where macroeconomic modelling can produce reliable directional guidance.

<Note>
  The 20-quarter forecast with full macroeconomic scenario modelling is available in the **Pro report**. The Basic report includes a shorter-range price trend indicator based on recent transaction history.
</Note>

## The Three Macroeconomic Scenarios

Rather than presenting a single price line, Estify models three scenarios that reflect genuinely different economic futures:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Base Case" icon="chart-line">
    The most likely outcome based on current economic conditions: moderate GDP growth, inflation returning toward the NBP target, and stable interest rates. Represents the central expectation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Optimistic" icon="arrow-trend-up">
    Driven by faster-than-expected rate cuts, strong wage growth, rising household purchasing power, and sustained demand in major urban centres. Prices rise faster than the base case.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Pessimistic" icon="arrow-trend-down">
    Models a deterioration in economic conditions: persistent inflation, rate increases, rising unemployment, or a significant reduction in housing demand. Prices grow more slowly or correct.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Each scenario is not a guess — it is built on a defined set of macroeconomic assumptions. You can see the key drivers behind each scenario in the report's methodology footnote.

## What Data Drives the Forecast?

Estify's price forecast model draws on data from **76 sources**, combining:

- **Transaction history**: actual registered sale prices for comparable properties in the same district and sub-market, going back up to 10 years
- **Macroeconomic indicators**: NBP (National Bank of Poland) interest rate decisions, CPI inflation data, GDP growth projections, and real wage trends
- **Local market dynamics**: the balance of supply (new completions, listings volume) and demand (mortgage applications, search activity) at the local level
- **Developer pipeline data**: planned new residential completions in the area, which can affect future supply and price pressure
- **Historical forecast calibration**: the model is continuously back-tested against actual price outcomes to improve accuracy quarter-on-quarter

## How to Read the Forecast Chart

In the Pro report, the forecast is presented as a chart with the following structure:

- **X-axis**: time, measured in quarters from the report date (Q1 through Q20)
- **Y-axis**: estimated price per square metre (PLN/m²) for the subject property's sub-market
- **Three lines**: base case (solid), optimistic (dashed, upper), pessimistic (dashed, lower)
- **Shaded band**: the confidence interval around the base case, which widens as the forecast extends further into the future — reflecting naturally increasing uncertainty

The spread between the optimistic and pessimistic lines tells you as much as the base case itself. A narrow spread signals a relatively stable local market. A wide spread suggests the location is sensitive to macroeconomic conditions and requires more conservative planning assumptions.

## Practical Example: Warsaw Apartment

Consider a 50 m² flat in Warsaw's Praga-Południe district, currently valued at 11,200 PLN/m² (560,000 PLN total). Under the three scenarios over 20 quarters:

| Scenario | Price/m² in 5 Years | Total Value | Appreciation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Optimistic | ~13,100 PLN/m² | ~655,000 PLN | +17% |
| Base Case | ~12,100 PLN/m² | ~605,000 PLN | +8% |
| Pessimistic | ~10,800 PLN/m² | ~540,000 PLN | −4% |

The base case projects approximately **8% appreciation over five years** — roughly 1.6% annually — consistent with long-run real price growth in established Warsaw neighbourhoods. The pessimistic scenario shows a modest nominal decline, which in real (inflation-adjusted) terms represents a more significant loss of purchasing power.

## Limitations and Caveats

Price forecasts are projections, not guarantees. There are things even the most sophisticated model cannot predict:

- **Sudden policy changes**: a major shift in Polish housing policy, mortgage subsidy programmes, or planning law can alter market dynamics quickly
- **Geopolitical shocks**: events that are, by definition, not visible in historical data
- **Hyper-local factors**: a specific street or building can diverge significantly from district-level trends due to local developments, building condition, or community changes
- **Black swan events**: low-probability, high-impact outcomes that sit outside all three modelled scenarios

Estify's forecast is a decision-support tool, not a financial guarantee. Treat it as one input alongside your own research, professional advice, and risk tolerance.

<Note>
  The forecast is most useful for **directional guidance** — understanding whether a market is broadly appreciating, plateauing, or at risk of correction — rather than for pinpointing an exact price at a specific future date. For investment decisions, the base case over 10–12 quarters is typically the most actionable planning horizon.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Read the price forecast alongside the **neighborhood profile section** of your report. A district with a forecast showing strong base-case appreciation but a neighborhood profile indicating declining amenity, ageing infrastructure, or falling population may warrant a more cautious interpretation. The two sections together give you a fuller picture than either does alone.
</Tip>
